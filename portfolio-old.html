Bio

I'm currently a security consultant and researcher intern for the Electronic Frontier Foundation. I'm part of a small team of technologists who watch for technologies that, by accident or design, pose a risk to computer users' freedoms—and then look for ways to fix them. We write code to make the Internet more secure, more open, and safer against surveillance and censorship. We explain technologies to lawyers and policymakers, and law and policy to the technology.

I'm also a Principal Consultant at Infosec consulting firm Glove Box, inc. I have more than a decade of experience in secure network design, penetration testing, incident response, forensics, and malware reverse engineering. I specialize in Computer Network Attack (CNA), and Computer Network Exploitation (CNE).

As a mostly self taught information security professional whose education began when I was 9 years old with my dad teaching him BASIC, my current focus is on formalizing my CS education with first a BS in Computer Science, and later a Masters.

Journal Paper Journal of Consumer Psychology, Volume 22, Issue 2, April 2012, Pages 191-194

Abstract

Although a substantial amount of research has examined the constructs of warmth and competence, far less has examined how these constructs develop and what benefits may accrue when warmth and competence are cultivated.
