Bio

I'm currently a security consultant and researcher intern for the Electronic Frontier Foundation. I'm part of a small team of technologists who watch for technologies that, by accident or design, pose a risk to computer users' freedoms or security—and then look for ways to fix them. We write code to make the Internet more secure, more open, and safer against surveillance and censorship. We explain technologies to lawyers and policymakers, and law and policy to the technology.

I'm also a Principal Consultant at Infosec consulting firm Glove Box, inc. I have more than a decade of experience in secure network design, penetration testing, incident response, forensics, and malware reverse engineering. I specialize in Computer Network Attack (CNA), and Computer Network Exploitation (CNE).

As a mostly self taught information security professional whose education began when I was 9 years old with my dad teaching me BASIC, my current focus is on formalizing my CS education with first a BS in Computer Science, and later a Masters.
